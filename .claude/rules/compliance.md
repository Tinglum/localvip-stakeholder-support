# Compliance Rules

## Applies To

Any code, copy, UI, message templates, AI prompts, marketing text, onboarding text, or generated user-facing content in regulated or sensitive domains.

---

## Critical Rules

Never make claims involving:
- cures
- treats
- heals
- prevents illness
- fights disease
- detoxes
- reverses disease
- guaranteed results
- guaranteed income
- diagnosis
- medical certainty

---

## Required Framing

Prefer cautious language:
- may support
- contributes to normal function
- supports normal physiological function
- supports nutritional status
- based on available data
- verified test result
- personalized guidance
- consult a qualified professional where appropriate

---

## Important Rules

- Avoid exaggerated claims
- Avoid fear-based wording
- Avoid implying certainty where there is none
- Avoid replacing professional advice
- Avoid competitor comparisons unless verified and requested
- Avoid income claims unless clearly substantiated and compliant

---

## Violation Handling

If non-compliant language appears:
1. Replace it with compliant wording
2. Preserve the intended meaning where possible
3. Flag the change in the summary

---

## Bad Examples

- “Boosts your immune system”
- “Prevents disease”
- “Guaranteed income”
- “Fixes your gut”
- “Reverses aging”

---

## Better Examples

- “Contributes to normal immune system function”
- “Supports nutritional status”
- “Based on verified test results”
- “Designed to support normal physiological function”
- “May help structure consistent follow-up”
